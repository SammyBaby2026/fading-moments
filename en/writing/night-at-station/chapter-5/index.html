The Night at the Station
Chapter 5

The Next Train

The train appeared as a ghost in the fog—first the light, then the sound, then the shape of something arriving from somewhere else. He watched it approach, feeling neither relief nor regret, only the strange calm that comes when a decision has been made for you.

The doors opened. Warmth spilled out onto the platform, carrying with it the smell of coffee and worn leather and other people's lives. He stood at the threshold, one foot on the platform, one on the step.

In his pocket, the letter felt heavier than it should. Three weeks of writing, a lifetime of things unsaid. He thought about what would happen if he sent it now. Would she read it? Would it change anything? Or would it simply be another message lost in the space between them?

The conductor looked at him with practiced patience. They had all night, his expression seemed to say. The mountains weren't going anywhere. The fog would lift eventually.

He stepped back onto the platform. The doors closed. The train pulled away, its taillights disappearing into the gray like a dream upon waking.

He walked back to the shelter, sat on the wooden bench, and waited. For the next train. For the fog to lift. For morning to come. For something he couldn't yet name.

The station master appeared with another cup of tea. They sat together in silence, two men who understood that some journeys are never finished, only paused. Outside, the snow continued to fall, each flake a small perfection that would soon be gone.

— The End —
