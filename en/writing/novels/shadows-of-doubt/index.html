Shadows of Doubt

Fifteen years after a steel mill explosion killed twenty-three workers, the survivors begin dying one by one. Detective Jack Morrison must confront his own past while hunting a killer who remembers everything.
Novel Mystery Noir Ongoing
Updated to Chapter 5 · 23,100 words · Est. 20 chapters, 70,000 words

About This Novel

Ashford, Pennsylvania — a dying steel town in America's Rust Belt, where the mills closed, the jobs vanished, and the American Dream rusted away. Fifteen years ago, the Harrison Steel Mill exploded on a cold November night, killing twenty-three night-shift workers. The official ruling: tragic accident. The truth: something far more sinister.

Now, a killer is hunting the survivors—one by one. Each death is staged as an accident. Each victim is found clutching a rusted gear marked with the number 15. The press calls him The Machinist.

Detective Jack Morrison (38) knows the truth. He was there that night—the only survivor who made it out. Now he must catch a killer who knows his secrets before the clock runs out. But the deeper Jack digs, the more he realizes that some doors, once opened, can never be closed. And the Machinist isn't just playing a game of murder—he's balancing the books of justice.

Investigative journalist Sarah Chen (34) lost her father in that explosion. She's spent fifteen years digging for the truth. Now she's Jack's only ally—and possibly his next weakness. Because in a town built on steel and secrets, trust is the most dangerous currency of all.
