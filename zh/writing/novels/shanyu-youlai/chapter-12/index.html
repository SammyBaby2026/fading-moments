Shadows of Doubt
第十二章：山雨欲来
2026年3月16日
4,500 words · 第12章，共20章

The storm 杰克 had been chasing for fifteen years was finally breaking overhead.

He stood in the parking garage beneath the federal building in Pittsburgh, watching rain sheet down the concrete walls in gray curtains. Marcus Pierce was upstairs, packing his bags for a midnight flight to Zurich. In three hours, he would be beyond the reach of American justice, living off the fortune he'd built on the bones of twenty-three dead men.

Unless 杰克 stopped him.

"You don't have to do this." 莎拉's voice came through his earpiece, tight with worry. She was three blocks away, monitoring police frequencies from a rented sedan. "The FBI agents are on their way. Twenty minutes, max. We can stall him until—"

"We don't have twenty minutes." 杰克 checked his watch: 10:47 PM. "His flight leaves at midnight. Once he's on that plane, he's gone. Extradition from Switzerland takes years, if it happens at all. And by then, the evidence will have disappeared. Witnesses will have accidents."

"So what are you going to do? Arrest him yourself? You're a suspended cop, 杰克. You have no badge, no authority. If you touch Pierce, you're committing assault."

"I'm not going to touch him." 杰克's hand moved to the pistol holstered at his hip—the service weapon he'd refused to surrender when they took his badge. "I'm going to talk to him."

"And if talking doesn't work?"

杰克 didn't answer. He couldn't answer, because he didn't know what he would do. Three days ago, he would have said that the law mattered more than vengeance. Three days ago, he would have trusted the system to work.

Three days ago, he hadn't known his father was murdered.

The elevator chimed. 杰克 stepped back into the shadows as the doors opened and Marcus Pierce emerged, rolling a black leather suitcase behind him. The lawyer looked different out of his element—without the bespoke suits and the corner office, he was just another aging man running from his sins. His face was drawn, his eyes red-rimmed from sleepless nights. He knew the net was closing. He just didn't know how close.

杰克 let him get halfway to his car—a silver Mercedes parked under a broken security light—before stepping out.

"Marcus."

Pierce spun, his hand diving into his coat. 杰克 had his weapon drawn before the lawyer could complete the motion.

"Don't." 杰克's voice was flat, empty. "I really don't want to shoot you. But I will if you make me."

Pierce's hand emerged slowly, empty. "Morrison. I should have known you'd be the one to find me." He managed a thin smile. "Your father had the same persistence. It got him killed."

"You admit it, then. You had him poisoned."

"I admit nothing. I'm simply acknowledging a pattern. Men who push too hard against the system tend to fall victim to unfortunate circumstances." Pierce's smile widened, though his eyes remained cold. "Liver failure, in your father's case. Very sad. He was drinking so heavily after the explosion—guilt will do that to a man. Or so I'm told."

杰克's finger tightened on the trigger. Just a fraction of an inch. So easy. One bullet, and Pierce would never smile again. Never kill again.

"You want to shoot me," Pierce said, reading his thoughts. "I can see it in your eyes. But you won't. Because you're still a cop, Morrison. Still one of the good guys. And good guys don't execute unarmed men in parking garages."

"I'm not a cop anymore. They took my badge, remember?"

"But you still think like one. You still believe in justice, in courts, in the rule of law. That's your weakness. It's why you'll lose." Pierce took a step closer, confident now. "The men I work for, they don't have such limitations. They do what needs to be done. They understand that power is the only law that matters."

"Who?" 杰克 demanded. "Who are you working for? Harrison's son? The senator?"

Pierce laughed. "You think it's that simple? One villain you can hunt down and arrest? There is no one man, Morrison. There's a system. A network of money and influence that stretches from Ashford to Washington to Zurich and back again. You can't fight a system with a badge and a gun."

"Maybe not." 杰克 lowered his weapon slightly. "But I can fight it with evidence. And testimony."

"What testimony? From whom?"

"From you."

Pierce's confidence faltered. "What?"

"You're going to testify. About everything. The payoffs, the cover-ups, the murders. You're going to name names, dates, account numbers. And in exchange, you'll get witness protection instead of life in prison."

"You're insane." Pierce backed away, shaking his head. "I would never—do you know what they'd do to me? The people I work for? They'd find me. They'd kill my family, my—"

"They're going to kill you anyway," 杰克 said quietly. "You know too much. You're a liability now. Why do you think they're sending you to Zurich? It's not to protect you. It's to silence you. Permanently."

Pierce stopped. For a moment, fear flickered across his face—real fear, not the theatrical kind he'd been performing.

"You have twenty seconds to decide," 杰克 said. "Testify, or die. Those are your only options."

Pierce opened his mouth to respond.

The shot came from somewhere above—from the stairwell, or the roof, or a window in the building across the street. 杰克 heard the crack, saw Pierce's head snap back, saw the spray of blood against the concrete wall. The lawyer crumpled, his body hitting the ground with a wet thud.

杰克 dove for cover behind a concrete pillar, weapon raised, scanning the shadows for the shooter. Nothing. No movement, no sound except the rain and his own heartbeat thundering in his ears.

"杰克!" 莎拉's voice, frantic in his earpiece. "What happened? I heard a shot!"

"Pierce is down. Sniper. Unknown location." 杰克 risked a glance at the body. Dead, definitely dead. Half his head was gone. "Call 911. And get backup here now."

"Already calling. 杰克, you need to get out of there. If the shooter is still—"

"I'm not leaving." 杰克 crept forward, staying low, using the parked cars for cover. "Pierce was going to talk. He knew who was behind everything. I need to find that shooter."

He reached the stairwell and took the stairs two at a time, weapon leading the way. The door to the roof was unlocked, swinging open in the wind. 杰克 burst through, sweeping the darkened rooftop with his pistol.

Empty.

Except for a single brass shell casing gleaming in the rain, and a note pinned to the door frame with a knife.

杰克 pulled the note free with trembling hands. The message was short, written in the same precise handwriting as all the others:

"Some debts can only be paid in blood. The game continues, Jackie. The final move is coming. —M"

杰克 crumpled the note in his fist, looking out over the rain-swept city. Pierce was dead. The last witness, silenced. The system had protected its own once again.

But The 机械师 was still out there. Still watching. Still playing his game.

And for the first time in fifteen years, 杰克·莫里森 wasn't sure which side he was on anymore.

The storm was here. And there was no shelter from it now.
